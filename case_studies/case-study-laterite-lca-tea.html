1. Split the feed

Dry laterite is converted to wet feed and partitioned into coarse and fine fractions using source-model split ratios.

2. Form the Mn stream

The coarse fraction passes through attrition and separation to isolate a small Mn-ox concentrate.

3. Estimate burdens

Slurry volume, FeSO4 demand, electricity, and heat define the main LCA contributors.

4. Annualize economics

Capital scaling, utilities, labour, feedstock, and product value roll into opex and margin.

Compact method

What is simplified and what remains faithful

Mass balance

The stream fractions are anchored to the original source model defaults. Feed is split into bulk coarse laterite, bulk fine laterite, attrition feed, fine fraction, heavy fraction, and Mn-ox concentrate using fixed process ratios.

LCA / GWP

The live GWP values are driven by FeSO4 demand, electricity allocation, bioleach heating, and nutrient medium. Emission factors are kept fixed so users can see the effect of operational choices rather than database changes.

Techno-economic analysis

The TEA uses a compact capex scaling model, annual operating hours, labour and feedstock assumptions, and a product-value estimate tied to the recovered laterite-derived product stream. It is an interactive case-study summary, not a bankable financial model.
